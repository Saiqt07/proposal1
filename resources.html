Guides and Tips for a Healthier Digital Life

Build better tech habits and achieve balance. Explore our practical, science-backed tips to boost your focus, productivity, and mental wellbeing in a digital world.

Digital Boundaries

Setting digital boundaries, like establishing tech-free zones or specific offline hours, helps individuals regain control over their time and reconnect meaningfully with people around them.1

The 20-20-20 Rule

Following the 20-20-20 rule — taking a 20-second break to look at something 20 feet away every 20 minutes — reduces digital eye strain and promotes better vision health.2

Curate Your Social Media

Curating your social media feeds to follow accounts that bring positivity and value protects your mental health from negativity and comparison traps.3

Intentional Technology Use

Being intentional with your technology use by setting goals before picking up your device prevents mindless scrolling and increases purposeful engagement.4

Digital Sabbaths

Taking regular digital sabbaths, such as spending one day each week away from screens, improves mindfulness and strengthens real-world connections.5

Sleep Hygiene

Prioritizing sleep hygiene by putting devices away at least an hour before bed leads to better sleep quality and deeper rest.6

Notification Management

Managing notifications by turning off non-essential alerts minimizes distractions and fosters deeper focus during important tasks.7

Screen Time Tracking

Using screen time tracking apps provides better awareness of your habits and helps you make conscious adjustments to your daily tech consumption.8

Tech-Free Bedroom

Creating a tech-free bedroom setup eliminates late-night scrolling temptations and supports a calming environment for rest.9

Single-Tasking

Practicing single-tasking — focusing fully on one task instead of multitasking — increases productivity and decreases mental fatigue.10

Scheduled Unplug Moments

Scheduling deliberate unplug moments throughout the day, such as walking outside without your phone, refreshes your brain and reduces stress.11

Digital Decluttering

Regularly decluttering digital spaces like your email inbox, downloads folder, and home screens minimizes overwhelm and boosts organization.12

Slow Technology

Embracing slow technology habits, such as sending thoughtful emails instead of quick texts, encourages deeper, more meaningful communication.13

Wellness-Focused Apps

Incorporating wellness-focused apps, like meditation, breathing exercises, or focus timers, supports healthier digital habits and mental resilience.14

Social Media Limits

Limiting social media usage by setting daily app limits prevents endless scrolling and helps reclaim time for more fulfilling offline activities.15
